Topics

Inner Planets

Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars orbit close to the Sun and complete revolutions relatively quickly.

Gas Giants

Jupiter and Saturn dominate with massive sizes and many moons; Uranus and Neptune are the ice giants.

Dwarf Planets

Smaller worlds like Pluto and Eris follow elongated paths and offer clues to the Solar System's history.

Orbits and Gravity

Gravitational forces set planets into orbital motion and keep them bound to the Sun.

Phases and Eclipses

Alignments produce lunar phases and, on rare occasions, solar and lunar eclipses.
